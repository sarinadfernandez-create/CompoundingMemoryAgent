## Incorporation

- When a draft plan significantly exceeds the reference in breadth and quality, flag only genuine gaps or mishandlings rather than inventing deficiencies — avoid penalizing thoroughness.
- When a draft already exceeds the reference in breadth and depth, focus review energy on verifying accuracy and completeness rather than adding items that may dilute clarity.

## International / KYC

- When a non-resident founder incorporates a US entity, always flag visa implications separately: owning a US company is legally distinct from performing physical work in the US, and the plan must explicitly note that working in the US without the appropriate visa is not permitted regardless of incorporation status.

## Conversion / Restructuring

- When advising on LLC-to-C-Corp conversions, always distinguish the F-reorganization as a named and distinct structural option alongside statutory conversion and merger/acquisition, since it often provides superior tax-attribute preservation and should be explicitly evaluated.

## Compliance & Tax

- Always prompt the customer to consider preservation of tax attributes such as net operating losses (NOLs) during entity restructuring, as the chosen conversion method can determine whether those attributes carry over or are lost.
