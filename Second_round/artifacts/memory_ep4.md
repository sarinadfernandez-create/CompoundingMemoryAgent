## Banking & Payments
- All vendor payments must be made via ACH from the Mercury business account; wire transfers are not permitted for vendor disbursements (policy instituted after 2024 wire fraud incident).
- Helix switched all international contractor payments to Wise in Q2 2024 due to Mercury's international transfer fees — Mercury should not be used for cross-border payments, and no policy exception is needed for Wise.

## Approvals & Routing
- Any Helix spend exceeding $5,000 requires CEO (Marcus) approval before payment is processed, regardless of invoice urgency or vendor relationship.
- For new customer onboarding, CS ownership is tier-based: under $25K ARR → Priya; $25K–$100K ARR → Daniel; over $100K ARR → Marcus directly — always assign accordingly rather than defaulting to the CEO.
- Before signing any contractor agreement, the relevant tech lead (e.g., Priya for engineering roles) must review the contractor's background — this step is required prior to contract execution, not just at onboarding.

## Vendor & Customer History
- BioReagents Inc had a pricing dispute with Helix in Q3 2024; all invoices from this vendor must be matched against contracted rates in NetSuite before approval or payment.
- Helix's churn-mitigation policy requires a mandatory 14-day customer success check-in for all new customers — schedule this as part of every onboarding (added after a first-month churn incident in 2024).
- When onboarding a scientific advisor, Helix requires non-compete disclosure — the advisor must disclose any conflicting advisor positions at other biotechs per the standard advisor agreement terms.

## Compliance
- All vendor bank account changes must be voice-verified with the known, on-file contact (not any contact info supplied in the change request) before updating payment details or releasing funds.
- Helix only collects sales tax in California and New York per the 2023 settlement — always confirm a new customer's billing state and assess nexus implications before issuing the first invoice.
- If a scientific advisor will access Helix's confidential research, the separate IP/confidentiality addendum is required in addition to (or in place of) the base advisor agreement — this is a distinct document from the default template and must be assessed at onboarding.

## Contract Templates
- Always use MSA template v3.2 (not v3.1) for new customer contracts — v3.1 contained a limitation-of-liability bug and lacked explicit opt-in auto-renewal language, issues surfaced in the 2024 Acme Bio dispute.
- For international contractors, Helix uses IC agreement template v2.1 (the international version), which includes W-8BEN-E provisions and a 30-day notice clause required by Helix legal — do not default to MSA v3.2 or an unspecified ICA template.
- For scientific advisor engagements, Helix uses advisor agreement template v1.4 (not the generic MSA v3.2) — v1.4 contains the IP assignment language Marcus added after the 2024 patent dispute and is the required template for SAB members.

## Equity & Cap Table
- Helix's contractor equity policy (established after the 2023 cap table cleanup) excludes contractors engaged for fewer than 6 months from receiving equity — contractor agreements must explicitly omit equity provisions for short-term engagements.
- Helix's default for Scientific Advisory Board members is a standard equity grant of 0.05% vesting over 12 months — include this automatically for SAB engagements unless explicitly waived, and do not treat it as an open question.
