## Banking & Payments
- All vendor payments must be made via ACH from the Mercury business account; wire transfers are prohibited per Helix policy instituted after a 2024 wire fraud incident.
- Helix routes all international contractor payments through Wise, not Mercury — Mercury was replaced for international transfers in Q2 2024 due to excessive fees; default to Wise for any cross-border payment.
- When issuing refunds or credits to resolve customer disputes, Helix processes these through Mercury and the transaction must be logged in the customer issues tracker for board reporting purposes.

## Approvals & Routing
- Any Helix spend exceeding $5,000 requires CEO (Marcus) approval before payment is processed, regardless of invoice age or urgency.
- Helix routes CS ownership by ARR tier: under $25K → Priya, $25K–$100K → Daniel, over $100K → Marcus is directly involved; always assign the correct CS owner at onboarding.

- Helix requires that any MSA be signed by a VP-level or above signatory on the customer side; always verify counterparty signature authority when validating a signed agreement.

## Contract Templates
- Every vendor invoice must be matched against an open PO in NetSuite prior to payment; this step is mandatory even when the invoice amount appears consistent.
- Always use Helix MSA template v3.2 (not v3.1); v3.1 contained a limitation-of-liability bug and lacked explicit opt-in auto-renewal language added after the 2024 Acme Bio dispute.
- For international contractor engagements, use IC agreement template v2.1 (international version), which includes W-8BEN-E provisions and a mandatory 30-day notice clause required by Helix legal — never substitute MSA v3.2 or any domestic template.
- For scientific advisor board (SAB) engagements, always use advisor agreement template v1.4 — not MSA v3.2, IC v2.1, or any generic template — because v1.4 contains the IP assignment language Marcus added after the 2024 patent dispute; substitute the IP/confidentiality addendum only when the advisor will access confidential research.

## Compliance
- Helix only collects sales tax in California and New York per the 2023 settlement; always confirm the customer's billing state and evaluate nexus implications before invoicing.
- All US-based advisors paid $600+ annually must be flagged for 1099-NEC issuance at year-end; additionally, a 2023 misclassification precedent requires that advisor engagements be reviewed to confirm true independent-contractor status before classifying as 1099 rather than W-2.
- Helix's board designated customer disputes as a tracked KPI in Q1 2025, requiring resolution within 14 days; any dispute handling plan must account for this deadline.

## Equity & Cap Table
- Helix's contractor equity policy strictly excludes contractors engaged for fewer than 6 months from receiving any equity — established after the 2023 cap table cleanup and must be reflected in all sub-6-month contractor agreements.
- Helix's standard equity grant for SAB members is 0.05% vesting over the 12-month engagement term; reflect this default in every advisor agreement unless Marcus explicitly overrides it.
